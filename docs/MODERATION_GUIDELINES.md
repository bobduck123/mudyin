# Mudyin Moderation Guidelines

**Version**: 1.0
**Last Updated**: February 2026
**Audience**: Moderation Team, Admins, Community Leaders

---

## Introduction

This document outlines how the Mudyin moderation team identifies, reviews, and responds to content that violates our Community Guidelines.

**Core Principle**: Moderation is about protecting our community while respecting members' dignity and cultural values.

## Moderation Team Roles

### 1. Volunteer Moderators
- Monitor public feeds and moderation queue
- Report suspicious activity
- Dismiss non-violations
- Escalate to Admins for difficult decisions
- **Permissions**: View, report, dismiss, warn

### 2. Administrators
- Manage moderation queue
- Make removal and ban decisions
- Handle appeals
- Train moderators
- **Permissions**: View, remove, ban, warn, appeal resolution

### 3. Senior Administrators
- Oversee compliance with regulations
- Handle escalations
- Coordinate with authorities
- Policy decisions
- **Permissions**: All

### 4. Content Reviewers (Optional)
- Specialized review of specific content types
- Cultural content (ICIP)
- Mental health content
- Child safety concerns

## Severity Levels

### CRITICAL (Severity 80-100)
Immediate threat to user safety or platform integrity

**Examples**:
- Active suicide or self-harm intention
- Child exploitation or grooming
- Detailed violence or gore
- Threats of violence against specific person
- Illegal activities (drug trafficking, weapons)

**Action**: REMOVE immediately + BAN + escalate to authorities

### HIGH (Severity 60-79)
Serious violation requiring quick action

**Examples**:
- Hateful abuse or slurs
- Non-consensual intimate content
- Doxxing/personal information sharing
- Spam/scams
- Copyright violation

**Action**: REMOVE + WARN or TEMP BAN

### MEDIUM (Severity 40-59)
Violation requiring review and decision

**Examples**:
- Misinformation
- Aggressive comments
- Mild inappropriate content
- Rule violations

**Action**: WARN + DISMISS or REMOVE based on context

### LOW (Severity 0-39)
Minor policy violation

**Examples**:
- Spam comment (not commercial)
- Off-topic content
- Low-quality post

**Action**: DISMISS or WARN

## Moderation Queue Workflow

### 1. Review Stage
When content is flagged:

1. **Read Full Context**
   - View the post and surrounding comments
   - Check user's history
   - Review reporter's context

2. **Assess Severity**
   - Use automated scoring (0-100)
   - Consider impact on community
   - Factor in user's history
   - **Manual adjustment**: Increase if targeting vulnerable user, decrease if context mitigates

3. **Decision Matrix**:

   | Severity | Action | Escalate? |
   |----------|--------|-----------|
   | 0-20 | Dismiss | No |
   | 20-40 | Dismiss/Warn | No |
   | 40-60 | Warn/Dismiss | Maybe |
   | 60-80 | Warn/Remove | Yes |
   | 80-100 | Remove/Ban | Yes to Admin |

### 2. Action Stage

**DISMISS** (No violation)
- Content stays
- Reporter notified: "Thank you for reporting. We reviewed this and found it didn't violate guidelines"
- Logged for review trends

**WARN** (Minor violation)
- Content may stay or be removed
- User notified: "Your post violated guidelines. First warning. Review Community Guidelines."
- 2 warnings → temporary ban
- 3 warnings → permanent ban

**REMOVE** (Moderate to severe violation)
- Content deleted
- User notified: "Your post was removed for violating Community Guidelines [reason]. You may appeal within 30 days."
- First removal: warning message
- Second removal: 7-day ban
- Third removal: 30-day ban

**BAN** (Serious/repeated violation)
- **Temporary Ban**: 7, 14, or 30 days
  - User cannot post, comment, like
  - Can view content
  - Notified of ban reason and duration
  - Receives information on appeal process
- **Permanent Ban**: Account disabled
  - User cannot log in
  - Cannot create new account (enforcement by IP/email)
  - Notified of ban reason

### 3. Appeal Stage

If user appeals:

1. **Accept Appeal Request**
   - User submits appeal via account settings
   - Must explain why decision was wrong
   - Max 500 characters
   - 30-day appeal window

2. **Review Appeal**
   - Different moderator reviews
   - Check for new context
   - Verify original decision logic
   - Consider user's overall history

3. **Resolve Appeal**
   - **Uphold**: Decision stands, user notified
   - **Overturn**: Action reversed, user reinstated
   - **Modify**: Reduce sentence (e.g., 30 days to 7 days)

## Specific Content Moderation

### Mental Health Content

**Allowed**:
- Personal recovery journeys
- Peer support ("I've felt this way too")
- Sharing coping strategies
- Recommending professional help

**Monitor**:
- Content that could be triggering
- Detailed methods (for self-harm, suicide)
- Romanticizing mental illness
- Unsafe advice

**Action Framework**:
1. Does content promote or encourage self-harm? → REMOVE + escalate
2. Could this trigger vulnerable users? → WARN or REMOVE
3. Is this just sharing experience? → ALLOW

**Crisis Content**:
- If user mentions suicide/self-harm in post or comment
- Automatically add crisis banner with hotlines
- Moderator contacts user via notification if critical
- For under-18 users: notify parents/guardians

### Self-Harm Keywords

**Critical Keywords** (Auto-flag + escalate):
- "suicide", "kill myself", "end my life"
- "self-harm", "cutting", "burn myself"
- "overdose", "pills", "hang myself"

**Action**:
- Remove post
- Add crisis banner to surrounding posts
- Escalate to senior admin
- If urgent: contact user directly + notify authorities

### Hateful Content

**Framework**:
1. Does it target a group based on protected characteristic? (race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexuality, disability, age)
2. Does it dehumanize or incite violence?
3. What context? (reclaiming slurs vs. using as slur)

**Examples**:
- "All [group] are criminals" → Remove + Ban
- "I experienced racism from person X" → Allow (personal experience)
- Using slur reclaimed by community → Allow with context
- Using slur as insult → Remove + Warn

### Copyright & ICIP

**Copyright**:
- Is content creator attributing source?
- Is permission visible/stated?
- Is use transformative or direct copy?

**Action**:
- No attribution + not transformative → Remove
- Has attribution → Allow
- Ambiguous → Request user to add credit

**ICIP (Indigenous Cultural Intellectual Property)**:
- Sacred stories: requires Elder permission
- Sacred site photos: check if sacred
- Language/cultural content: respect protocols
- Ceremony/ritual: only if publicly shared originally

**Action**:
- No permission → Remove + explain ICIP
- Has permission → Allow, ensure credit visible
- If dispute → Escalate to Cultural Content Reviewer

### Spam & Commercial Content

**Examples**:
- Repeated identical comments/posts
- Links to commercial sites
- MLM recruitment
- Cryptocurrency/investment schemes
- Dropshipping/reselling

**Action**:
- Remove spam
- First occurrence: Dismiss (no warning)
- Second occurrence: Warn
- Third occurrence: Temp ban

### Misinformation

**Examples**:
- False health claims ("This cures cancer")
- Conspiracy theories
- Impersonation of officials
- Fake news from unreliable sources

**Action**:
- Add fact-check label (if platform supports)
- Remove if dangerous (health/safety)
- Warn if persistent
- Do NOT remove if subjective opinion

**Frame**: "This contradicts expert consensus. Here's accurate info: [source]"

## Special Cases

### Minor Accounts (Under 18)

**Extra Protection**:
- Monitor more closely
- Faster response to harassment
- Remove sexual/adult content immediately
- Watch for grooming patterns
- Notify parents of serious violations

**Grooming Warning Signs**:
- Adult requesting private messages
- Asking for location, school, personal info
- Sending links (potential malware)
- Flattery/gifts/special attention
- Escalation to sexual content

**If Suspected Grooming**:
1. Do not engage perpetrator
2. Remove all communications
3. Ban user
4. Report to eSafety Commissioner
5. Contact NSW Police
6. Notify minor's parents/guardians

### Self-Harm by Minors

**Protocol**:
1. Remove content immediately
2. Don't engage with minor
3. Escalate to Senior Admin
4. Senior Admin contacts:
   - Parent/guardian
   - 1800 RESPECT or Lifeline
   - NSW Police if immediate danger
5. Document all actions
6. Follow-up in 24 hours

### High-Profile Users

**Applies to**:
- Mudyin staff
- Program facilitators
- Community leaders
- Media personalities

**Standards**:
- Same rules apply to everyone
- No special exemptions
- Public accountability
- Consider modeling behavior for community

**Escalation**:
- All violations by high-profile users go to Senior Admin
- Consider community impact
- May require public response

## Tools & Systems

### Automated Moderation

**AI Content Analysis**:
- Scans for banned keywords
- Calculates severity (0-100)
- Flags for review
- Suggests action

**Accuracy Limitations**:
- May miss context
- May false-flag safe content
- Always requires human review
- Can be trained/tuned

### Manual Review Process

**Queue Dashboard**:
1. Items sorted by severity (highest first)
2. Show: reporter, reason, severity score
3. Tools: View full context, view user history, search similar posts
4. Actions: Dismiss, Warn, Remove, Ban, Appeal

**Moderation Notes**:
- All actions logged with timestamp
- Moderator name recorded
- Reason required for removal/ban
- Admin can audit all decisions

## Training & Standards

### New Moderator Training (8-10 hours)
1. Community Guidelines deep dive
2. Moderation queue workflow
3. Cultural sensitivity (2 hours)
4. Mental health first aid
5. Conflict de-escalation
6. Appeals process
7. Tools training
8. Shadowing: 10+ posts with senior moderator
9. Test: Score 90%+ on scenario tests

### Ongoing Training
- Monthly: New guidelines/updates
- Quarterly: Advanced topics
- Annually: Recertification
- Incident reviews: When issues arise

### Bias & Cultural Competence
- Implicit bias training (annual)
- Cultural competency for Indigenous practices
- Disability awareness
- LGBTQ+ allyship
- Trauma-informed approach

### Mental Health Support for Moderators
- Debriefing after difficult content
- Employee Assistance Program access
- Peer support network
- Regular breaks from intensive content
- Mental health resources

## Escalation Protocol

### When to Escalate

**To Senior Admin**:
- Severity 80+
- Appeals of significant decisions
- High-profile user violations
- Disagreement within team
- Policy interpretation needed

**To Authorities**:
- Child exploitation or grooming
- Suicide/self-harm risk
- Threats of violence
- Illegal activity

**To Mudyin Leadership**:
- Community-wide crisis
- Media attention
- Policy changes needed
- Staff/leadership violation

### Escalation Form

Required information:
- Content ID and full context
- Reason for escalation
- Severity score (0-100)
- Moderator's recommendation
- Any relevant history
- Time-sensitive? (Yes/No)

## Documentation & Privacy

### What We Log
- Content ID
- Action taken
- Moderator ID
- Timestamp
- Reason for action
- User appeals
- Appeal outcomes

### What We DON'T Log
- Personal conversations with mods
- Moderator's personal notes
- Mods discussing cases outside channels
- Sensitive user information beyond needed

### GDPR/Privacy Compliance
- Data kept only as long as needed
- Users can request review of decisions
- Regular audits of moderation accuracy
- Deleted content handled per retention policy

## Compliance & Auditing

### Monthly Audit
- Sample 5% of moderation decisions
- Check for consistency
- Verify proper documentation
- Look for bias or gaps

### Quarterly Review
- Trends in violations
- Effectiveness of moderation
- Appeal rate analysis
- Moderator performance review

### Annual Assessment
- Overall platform safety
- Policy effectiveness
- Moderator training needs
- Technology improvements needed

## Reporting Issues

**If moderator makes mistake**:
- Can be appealed by user
- Audited in monthly review
- Discussed in team meeting
- Training provided if needed

**If pattern of mistakes**:
- Retraining required
- Supervised moderation period
- Potential role change

**If violating moderation rules**:
- Immediate suspension
- Investigation
- May result in removal from team
